Intro

Choral is a platform that allows musicians/artists/anybody, to share short snippets of lyrics or a poem to display their work. On Choral you will be able to post lyrics, reply to a post, "like", "follow" and more! Choral aims to help a community to create more and provide inspiration.

Choral will create a platform where like-minded artists can come together in making a piece of art. Music is universal, that means this magic is everywhere, we just need somewhere to put it. Creativity is meant to be unleashed and can only do so when given the right tools. Choral will be the perfect example of what can go right when a music-driven developer is behind it.

Technologies Used:

Adobe Illustrator, MongoDB, ExpressJS, AngularJS and NodeJS

Logo Iterations

It seems as though I can never find the perfect fit, so naturally, I just keep making and making until I can't make anymore and I end up with several different options. Logos for me are one of the best parts of creating something new because there are so many directions in which you can go! Now, the project ended up being named "Choral", but as you can see from the photos, it was originally going to be called "Chorus".



After "Chorus" turned into "Choral" I decided to play around with the word and create something that both embodied the message of what the application does and keep it fun. I created two different versions and played around with the idea of a "dark" and "light" version.

Branding

Colors

The primary color pallette will be used for the branding of Choral. It has been designed to give a calm and peaceful vibe. This color pallette works well online and can be used in variation.


The secondary color pallette will be used for the text used in Choral. These colors will act as the base to help set the tone. This pallette can be used for headings, subheadings, and captions, but will not be used for links.

Project Phases

Desktop v1

Description:

In the early stages of Choral, the main color was purple! I tried to keep it simple and made the forms for Choral, simple and easy to use so that the user feels welcome. Also, the card view is kept clean with a single card view. The actions are put right on the card for instant access to engagement.

Desktop v2

Description:

In version 2, I made the cards 'fancy' with the font. I made it 'brighter' by throwing in a bit more white and then for card display, I placed them in a grid which will have an infinite scroll. I still kept the single card view, with a bit more emphases with darkening the background. Finally, on the users profile I tie it together again with the grid displaying their own lyric cards.
