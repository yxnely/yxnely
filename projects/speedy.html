Summary:

Intro

Ever have one of those days where you get everything done? Nah, neither have we...until now. Meet Speedy, a delivery application that will help fulfill your needs when it comes to getting items to your door. All of this power at the touch of your fingertips…or mouse. We're all busy, whether you need to get to that next meeting or pick up the kids from school, we're here for you. Speedy will give you the full advantage of being able to stay on the go and save you time! No more running around in circles thinking how you’re going to get things done. Let us get them done for you.

Technologies Used:

Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, MarvelApp and good 'ol pen and paper

Planning

Plannning is one of my favorite parts of the process. I really enjoy sitting down, clearing my head and getting my thoughts out on pen and paper. Sometimes it just flows out, but other times it gets a bit hard to get it out. Whatever the challenge is, Speedy made it fun since I had never designed a 'delivery' type app.

Sketching

I knew that from the start I would have to think about what pages exactly I would need, outlining these items help smooth the process and flow that the application would take. Afterwards, sketching out my ideas became easier with set goals in mind.

Project Phases

With the phases, I tried to break up my time into chunks and determine what would happen where. This helped with keeping me accountable to deadlines which kept the process moving.

Description:

After reviewing and recieving feedback, I decided that the best would be to simplify the page. Although there was not much going at first, I still then saw that I could simplify the process even further. I accomplished this with:

  • Using one main color of 'blue' throughout the page.
  • Reduced the menu items yet still keeping the important parts.
  • Changing the sign up process so that it is uni-directional making it more straightforward.
  • Yes...still lots of blue.

Mobile

Description:

After even more iterations and more feedback, Speedy quickly morphed into its better and final state. You may notice a couple of things such as a more minimalistic view as well as a color change! The blue was getting overwhelming and green seemed to fit better as green conveys 'growth', 'freshness' and most importantly for Speedy, 'safety'. We like for our users to find comfort in using our application and feel like the vibrant energy of the green portrays just that.
